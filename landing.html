The Problem

Event-Driven Congestion Is
Hard to Predict. Harder to Manage.

Political rallies, festivals, sports events, and sudden gatherings create localized traffic breakdowns that today's systems cannot anticipate.

🚨

No Advance Quantification

Event impact is never measured before deployment. Police rely on experience, not data — leading to under- or over-deployment of resources.

📊

Experience-Driven Decisions

Resource deployment depends on individual officer knowledge. No standardized framework exists for barricade counts, personnel needs, or diversion routes.

🔁

Zero Post-Event Learning

After each incident resolves, nothing is captured. The system resets — the same mistakes happen at the next rally, flood, or road closure.

📍

No Spatial Intelligence

Which junctions are highest risk? Which zones overflow first? Without geospatial density analysis, traffic command is flying blind.

Pipeline

From Raw Data to Actionable Command

Five automated stages convert raw incident logs into forecasts, resource plans, and diversion routes.

1

Ingest & Clean

Raw event CSV is loaded, datetime-parsed, coordinates validated for Bengaluru bounds, and duplicates removed.

2

Feature Engineering

Temporal features (hour, weekday), zone/junction frequencies, and Haversine spatial density within 500m radius added.

3

Risk Scoring

Weighted multi-factor score (0–100) computed using road closure, priority, cause, duration, and area density.

4

ML Training

Random Forest classifier predicts risk level. Random Forest regressor forecasts disruption duration in minutes.

5

Recommend & Route

Resource optimizer and diversion engine generate deployment sheets and routing strategies ready for field dispatch.
